Terry grew up in South London in an immigrant family. He went to one of the worst-performing schools in the borough — but refused to let that define him. He earned A-stars, secured a place at a top sixth form, and went on to study law at the University of Warwick with a magic circle firm offer on the table.

But a conversation at his church changed everything. Someone introduced him to Airbnb as a business model, and Terry saw an opportunity most people walk straight past. His first investment was £8.5K — not from savings, but raised entirely through warm outreach to people already in his life.

From that single deal, he built a 14-unit portfolio before graduating. No inheritance. No wealthy parents. No shortcuts — just a system that works, and the discipline to follow it.
