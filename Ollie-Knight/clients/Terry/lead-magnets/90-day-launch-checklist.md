# The 90-Day Airbnb Launch Checklist

### Every Step From Zero to Your First Profitable Airbnb, Tick Them Off as You Go

**By Terry Dwobeng | Property Labs**

---

Look, I'm not going to sugarcoat it. Most people spend months "researching" Airbnb and never actually launch. This checklist is the exact sequence I'd follow if I was starting from scratch today. No theory. No fluff. Just the steps, in order, with a box to tick when you've done them.

Print this out. Stick it on your wall. And start ticking.

---

## PHASE 1: CREDIBILITY, Weeks 1-2

*Before you touch a property, you need to know your stuff and look like you know your stuff.*

- [ ] Research your target area (shortlist 3 cities or boroughs)
- [ ] Analyse 10 existing Airbnb listings in each area (note pricing, photos, reviews)
- [ ] Calculate average nightly rates and occupancy for your target areas
- [ ] Complete a property management basics course (free ones count)
- [ ] Set up your business Instagram or social media presence
- [ ] Create your investor credibility one-pager (who you are, what you do, your plan)
- [ ] Join 3 local property networking groups (Facebook, Meetup, or in-person)
- [ ] Attend at least 1 property meetup or event (show your face, collect contacts)

**End of Phase 1 checkpoint:** You can confidently explain your Airbnb strategy to anyone in under 2 minutes.

---

## PHASE 2: CAPITAL, Weeks 3-5

*You don't need a mortgage. You need a plan and the right conversations.*

- [ ] List 20 people in your network who might invest or lend
- [ ] Practise your pitch with a trusted friend (get honest feedback)
- [ ] Approach your first 5 potential investors
- [ ] Follow up with everyone who said "let me think about it"
- [ ] Secure your first investment commitment
- [ ] Open a business bank account (keep everything separate from personal)
- [ ] Get a basic legal agreement template (joint venture or loan agreement)
- [ ] Total capital target: **£5k-£25k secured**

**End of Phase 2 checkpoint:** Money is committed and sitting in a business account, ready to deploy.

---

## PHASE 3: PROPERTY, Weeks 6-8

*This is where most people stall. Don't overthink it. Run the numbers, make a decision, move.*

- [ ] Contact 10 letting agents in your target area
- [ ] View at least 5 properties
- [ ] Run each one through the Deal Analyser (if it doesn't stack, move on)
- [ ] Negotiate terms (rent-to-rent or management agreement)
- [ ] Secure your first property
- [ ] Get landlord permission for short-term lets (in writing)
- [ ] Check local council regulations and any licensing requirements
- [ ] Arrange insurance (short-term let cover, not standard landlord insurance)

**End of Phase 3 checkpoint:** You have keys in your hand and a signed agreement.

---

## PHASE 4: LAUNCH, Weeks 9-11

*Speed matters here. The faster you go live, the faster you earn. Don't wait for perfect.*

- [ ] Furnish the property (budget: £2k-£4k, no more)
- [ ] Professional photography, or use your iPhone with good natural lighting
- [ ] Write a compelling Airbnb listing title and description
- [ ] Set competitive pricing (first 5 bookings at a discount to build reviews)
- [ ] Create a house manual and guest welcome pack
- [ ] Set up your cleaning system (reliable cleaner on speed dial)
- [ ] Automate messaging (check-in instructions, check-out reminders)
- [ ] **Go live on Airbnb + Booking.com**

**End of Phase 4 checkpoint:** Your listing is live and bookable. You're in business.

---

## PHASE 5: CASH FLOW, Week 12

*Now prove the model works. Track everything.*

- [ ] First booking confirmed
- [ ] First 5-star review received
- [ ] Monthly P&L tracked in a spreadsheet (revenue minus all costs)
- [ ] Systems documented so someone else could run it
- [ ] Plan for unit #2 started

**End of Phase 5 checkpoint:** You have a profitable, running Airbnb and a clear path to the next one.

---

## Terry's Quick Rules

These are non-negotiable. I learned them the expensive way so you don't have to.

1. **Never spend more than £4k furnishing a 2-bed.** You're not designing a show home. You're building a business.
2. **Target minimum 65% occupancy.** Below that, your numbers won't work long-term.
3. **If the deal doesn't make £500/month profit, walk away.** There are plenty of deals. Don't force one that's marginal.
4. **Always have a 3-month cash reserve.** Things break. Bookings dip. Be ready.
5. **Your first unit is the hardest, it gets easier after that.** The skills, systems, and confidence you build on unit one carry straight into unit two.
