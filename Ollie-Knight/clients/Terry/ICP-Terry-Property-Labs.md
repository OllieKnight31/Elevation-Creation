## 1 - North-Star ICP Statement

I help junior corporates or late-stage university students getting into corporate to start and run a profitable Airbnb company within 90 days, achieving a minimum of one profitable property. We also teach raising finance methods for those who do not have enough capital to start, so they can build passive income, escape the corporate grind, and create the time, financial, and location freedom they desire.

## 7 - Objection Handling Matrix

| Objection (Exact Quote) | Hidden Fear / Root Cause | Counter-Move |
|---|---|---|
| **I do not have the money right now** | Scared they will not make their return at a quick enough rate. Biggest bet they have ever made on themselves. | We teach raising finance strategies. When Terry started, he did not have money to start - he financed getting onto the program AND his first Airbnb project. This is exactly what we teach in the program. |
| **Give me two or three months, I can save up and come back** | Want a cushion and comfortability before putting money into program or Airbnb. Delay tactic. | Realistically, when we leave this call, you are not going to fixate on joining for the next 2-3 months. You will forget about your goals. Best time to start Airbnb was years ago. Second best time is now. Every month you delay is a month you lose money. If your dream is 5,000 GBP/month, three months delay = 15,000 GBP lost. |
| **Airbnbs look saturated to me** | Do not think they will succeed when others are doing it. Fear of competition. | Yes, people are starting Airbnbs, but it is met by increased demand. Airbnbs are growing faster than hotels. Wherever there are people, there is need for housing - Airbnb cannot be saturated. Many people do it badly - search Airbnb now and you will see horror listings. There is always space for someone to do it properly. |
| **I need to speak to my parents** | Need validation before making big decision. May not have full autonomy over finances. | Understand and respect that. Book a follow-up call. Offer to answer any questions they or their parents have about the program. |
| **I am scared this will not work** | Do not take chances on themselves. Probably the largest amount they have spent in their life. No history of taking risks. | Share Terry's story - started as uni student with no money. Show proof of client results. Explain the raising finance module reduces risk. Clear ROI timeline of 45-90 days. |
| **Can I think about it?** | Hesitation. The moment of motivation is passing. Want to delay decision. | What specifically do you need to think about? Address the real concern. Remind them that delay costs money and opportunity. |

## 10 - Negative ICP (Anti-Persona)

| Trait | Why Bad Fit | Funnel Routing |
|---|---|---|
| **Get-Rich-Quick Expectations** | Expect to earn 10,000 GBP/month immediately. Too high expectations. When they realize results take work, they become a hassle and issue. | Screen heavily on sales call. Set realistic expectations. If they cannot accept 90-day timeline, not a fit. |
| **Less Than 1,000 GBP Liquid** | Cannot afford program AND starting capital. Will be stressed and desperate for results. High chargeback risk. | Qualify finances early. Minimum 5,000 GBP liquid required (after program cost). |
| **No Monthly/Weekly Income** | Only have liquid cash with nothing coming in. Will be suffocated if results take time. Cannot sustain during slow season. | Verify they have ongoing income source. All savings with no income = disqualify. |
| **Does Not Take Accountability** | Blames everybody around them and environment for why they are not successful. Will not implement and will blame Terry when results do not come. | Listen for victim language on calls. If blaming others for current situation, likely will blame coach too. |
| **Too Passive / Avoids Difficult Conversations** | Not ready to put it all on the line. Not an action taker. Will not do the outreach and landlord calls required. | Assess energy and commitment level on sales call. Ask about past times they took action on goals. |
| **Way Too Eager / Pushy** | Messaging multiple times, trying to speed up process. Puts literally everything they have into program and expects the world in return. | Red flag behavior pre-sale indicates problem client. If too eager before paying, worse after. |
| **Unrealistic Ambitions** | Example: Client said they want to earn an airport. Charged back after two months. Fantasy expectations vs. realistic business building. | Ask about realistic goals on sales call. If answer is completely detached from reality, disqualify. |
| **Under 18 Years Old** | Cannot legally sign contracts. May not have maturity for business decisions. | Verify age during qualification. Must be 18+. |

## 12 - Final Gut-Check & Next Steps

### Strategic Considerations

- **Primary ICP:** Junior corporate earning 3,000+ GBP/month with 10,000 GBP liquid saved. Business-savvy since young. Dream is to run their own business. Similar age to Terry.
- **Secondary Market:** University students - Terry has unique positioning here as he built this as a uni student. Competitors are tapping this market but Terry has authentic credibility.
- **Key Differentiator:** Terry started with no money as a uni student. Authentic story. Teaches raising finance from personal experience. Relatable to young aspiring entrepreneurs.
- **Biggest Fear:** May miss some people by focusing on corporates. Uni student market is untapped advantage. But if forced to choose: corporates over uni students because of higher financial capacity.

### Ideal Client Summary

If Terry could only work with ONE type of person for the next year:

A junior corporate earning a good salary (3,000 GBP/month+) with 10,000 GBP liquid saved after purchasing the program. Someone who has been business-savvy since they were young - always into small businesses. Their dream is not just passive income, but to actually run a business. Why? Because it is very similar to Terry himself. The age gap is not too big. They already have money liquid so no need to worry about finding investors - they can start immediately. They have income coming in every month so the program has not suffocated them. And they already know a thing or two about business because it is something they are naturally passionate about.
